Roast Beef Tenderloin with Red Wine & Shallot Sauce

4 pounds beef tenderloin premium oven roast
3 tablespoons black peppercorns, coarsely crushed
1 teaspoon salt
3 tablespoons olive oil
SAUCE:
4 tablespoons butter
2 cups sliced shallots
1 teaspoon granulated sugar
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 tablespoon minced fresh thyme
3-1/2 cups beef stock
1/2 cup dry red wine
1/4 cup brandy
1 bay leaf
2 tablespoons cornstarch


Sprinkle tenderloin with peppercorns and salt, pressing to adhere. In roasting pan, heat oil over medium-high heat; sear tenderloin all over. Roast in 375ºF oven for about 55 mins. Transfer to cutting board. Tent with foil; let stand for 15 mins. before carving.

SAUCE: Meanwhile, in saucepan, heat 2 tbsp. of the butter over medium heat; cook shallots, stirring occasionally, for about 10 mins. or until softened. Stir in sugar; cook for about 10 mins. or until shallots are golden and caramelized. Stir in garlic and thyme; cook for 5 mins. Add beef stock, wine, brandy and bay leaf; bring to a boil. Reduce heat; simmer for about 30 mins. or until sauce is reduced to about 2 cups. Whisk cornstarch with 1 tbsp. water; whisk into sauce and boil until thickened and smooth. Discard bay leaf. Add roasting pan juices to sauce. Bring to boil; whisk in remaining butter. Serve with roast. Enjoy!
